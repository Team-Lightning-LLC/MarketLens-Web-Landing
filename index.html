MarketLens

Beyond Retrieval

Every AI product on the market does the same thing. You type a question, it searches the web, it gives you paragraphs with footnotes. Some do it faster. Some cite better. All of them stop at exactly the point where the real work begins. And all of them bury what they produce in a conversation you’ll never find again.

The real work is analysis. Decomposing a question into its constituent dimensions. Searching each one with source discipline. Detecting gaps in your own coverage. Searching again. Then synthesizing everything—not stacking it, connecting it—into a document that holds up under scrutiny.

That process takes a skilled analyst six to eight hours. It costs between five hundred and fifteen hundred dollars in labor. It degrades with fatigue. It depends on the analyst remembering to check the angles that don’t occur to them naturally.

MarketLens is the research process at your fingertips.

What gets delivered

A structured, multi-page analytical report. Named, organized, and retrievable—not buried in a chat history. A document with sections, frameworks, evidence, and citations. The kind you’d hand to a client, present to a board, or build a position on.

Sourcing Every claim is traceable to its source. Full URLs, MLA citations, complete bibliography. Recency-constrained and fully verified.

Frameworks From Risk Assessment and Scenario Modeling to Ecosystem Mapping, Competitive Positioning, and your own custom frameworks — MarketLens generates reports tailored to what matters to you.

Self-directed gap analysis The system audits its own coverage, identifies what’s missing, and goes back for more. Your analyst doesn’t do this.

Workspaces, not chat logs Your research lives in named documents inside organized projects. Follow up on any report. Branch into new directions. Nothing gets lost in a scroll of conversations.

The Architecture

MarketLens is built by people who’ve spent their lives studying how markets work to understand the underlying dynamics that generate change. MarketLens was designed to understand more completely complex questions like how companies and industries evolve, how relationships between sectors shift, and how the uncertainty beneath a given market influences decision making.

Every research platform on the market is built to cover what’s happening. Not one of them is built to answer what you’re actually asking — how your client’s holdings could respond to a shifting macro environment, whether two companies in your portfolio provide true diversification, or where the real weaknesses are in your largest position. MarketLens doesn’t publish coverage. It runs the analysis you need, on the question you have, when you need it.

Your first report is ten minutes away.
